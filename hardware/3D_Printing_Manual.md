# 3D Printing Manual

This manual describes the basic procedure used to prepare and print drone parts with a Bambu Lab 3D printer. Follow the printer manufacturer's instructions whenever they differ from this guide.

## 1. Prepare the printer

Before starting a print:

- remove loose filament, failed-print debris, and other objects from the print chamber;
- check that the build plate is installed correctly and seated flat;
- inspect the nozzle for visible residue or blockage;
- enable the air-filtration system when required;
- check the rear filament path and waste chute if the printer reports a blockage;
- confirm that the selected build plate, nozzle, and filament profile match the installed hardware.

> **Safety:** Do not touch the nozzle, build plate, or recently printed parts until they have cooled to a safe temperature. Clean the nozzle only according to the printer manufacturer's instructions. Use protective gloves when handling rough, cut, or damaged carbon-fibre-filled parts.

## 2. Import and inspect the model

1. Open the STL file in the slicing software.
2. Confirm that the model has the intended dimensions.
3. Orient the part to reduce unsupported overhangs and improve structural strength.
4. Check that the model fits completely within the printable area.
5. Add supports, brims, or other adhesion features where required.
6. Confirm that the selected filament and process profiles are appropriate for the part.

## 3. Slice the model

Select **Slice plate** after checking the model orientation and print settings.

After slicing, inspect the preview for:

- missing layers or incomplete geometry;
- unsupported sections;
- unexpected gaps;
- excessive support material;
- estimated print time and material use.

Return to the preparation view and adjust the settings if the preview reveals a problem.

## 4. Send the print

When the sliced preview is satisfactory, select the print menu and choose the appropriate option.

Depending on the available setup, the sliced file may be:

- sent directly to the printer;
- exported for transfer to the printer; or
- printed from the current plate.

Before confirming the print, verify the selected printer, build plate, filament, and process profile.

## 5. Monitor the first layers

Observe the beginning of the print and confirm that:

- filament is extruded consistently;
- the first layer adheres to the build plate;
- the model does not shift;
- no filament accumulates around the nozzle;
- the printer does not report a feed, chute, or build-plate error.

Stop the print if the first layer fails or if the printer behaves unexpectedly.

## 6. Remove and inspect the part

After the print finishes:

1. allow the build plate and printed part to cool;
2. remove the part without applying excessive force;
3. remove supports or brims carefully;
4. inspect the part for warping, cracks, weak layer adhesion, blocked holes, and dimensional errors;
5. confirm that screws and connected components fit as intended.

For drone parts, also confirm that motor mounts, arm connections, and landing-leg interfaces are sufficiently rigid.

## 7. Clean the printer after use

After the printer has cooled:

- remove loose filament and print debris;
- clean the build plate using an appropriate method;
- check that the nozzle area and waste chute are clear;
- leave the print chamber and surrounding workspace clean.

## Expected output

At the end of this procedure, the printed part should:

- match the intended dimensions;
- show no major warping or cracks;
- have usable mounting holes and interfaces;
- be sufficiently rigid for its intended role;
- be ready for assembly or further finishing.
